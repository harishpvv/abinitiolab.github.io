About

The objective of this site is to familiarise new students, researchers and enthusiastic people about abinitio methods of predicting material properties. It provides information about, theoretical models,  opensource tools, latest and important findings of abinitio methods at classical and quantum level. 

Click to edit text

Way of approaching....


  • First get yourself familiarize with mathematical tools required to do computational materials science. For text reference you can follow Mathematical Physics by Prof. V. Balakrishnan, IIT Madras.
  • Next, you should understand little bit about mechanics which tells, how a system evolves  given initial positions and velocities of particles in the system, then thermodynamics which tells how matter interacts with energy and vice-versa macroscopically.
  • After understanding the mathematical tools and dynamical laws its time to know how matter looks like at nano meter scale i.e crystal structure (in this site we only talk materials science about crystalline materials).
  • After above steps, you can start with the important parts like QE, DFT etc.,
